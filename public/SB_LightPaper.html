How It Works

üîë Core Innovation: Committed Generosity

‚ñº

Seedbase introduces seed ($CIK): a digital token that represents committed value.

Key Properties:
  • Committed on Seedbase ‚Äî Platform buys and locks seed, removing it from circulation
  • Cannot be moved ‚Äî Locked until commitment period ends
  • Cannot be spent ‚Äî Only surplus flows to missions
  • Can be committed ‚Äî For 1-5 years, or permanently

How It Works:

  1. You commit USDC ‚Üí it converts to seed ($CIK) at current rate
  2. Seed locks in the Vault
  3. That locked value creates capacity for missions
  4. As the network grows, seed appreciates
  5. Appreciation creates surplus
  6. Surplus funds real-world impact

üèóÔ∏è The Architecture 5 Components

‚ñº

1. The Vault

Where committed seed lives. Once locked, seed cannot be moved, sold, or spent until the commitment period ends.

2. The Ledger

The calculation and distribution engine. Continuously measures locked seed, time, network growth, and surplus‚Äîthen automatically distributes to missions and participants.

3. Seedbases

Community anchors for missions. Represent long-term committed seed, communities around shared values, and collective capacity to create change.

4. Keys (Participation Roles)

Non-transferable credentials that define how people participate: Activators, Trustees, and Envoys.

5. Provision Pools

Flexible, transparent funding pools where communities can accept donations, tag withdrawals, and maintain accountability.
